A claude fable 5 review notes the following:

The veto mechanism is fine — recognize what it is. Since the override
threshold equals the original threshold, the veto is really a
motivated second reading: it buys a pause, a written justification,
and a paper trail, not a block. That's a good, self-limiting
design. Its only cost is explanation, which the texts already pay.

Entrenchment by article number is fragile. Article 19's three-quarters
list ("articles 4, 8, 9, 12, 15, 19, 33, 37, 38, 40") silently breaks
if any renumbering ever occurs. No edit — just adopt the discipline of
never renumbering (use "Article 6 bis"-style insertions, as the RI
already does).

Every AGO is a full election. Annual half-renewal means the complete
electoral machinery — commission électorale, CNIL-informed e-voting,
the 45/30/21/14/10/7-day timeline — runs every single year. This is
the heaviest recurring burden in the texts. I considered proposing
relief and decided against it: staggered mandates are worth it, the
proportionality language in RI article 17 is the right safety valve,
and RI articles 10 and 19 already confine nullity to irregularities
that could change outcomes — the drafting anticipated the "imperfect
compliance" problem well. But go in with open eyes: the AG timeline is
the piece most likely to be missed in practice, so put it in a
checklist, not just in the RI.

Voting opens before debate. Members can vote seven days before hearing
anyone speak. The revote-until-close rule and article 16's
text-stability rules mitigate this honestly, and the participation
gain is real. I flag it only so the tradeoff is chosen, not inherited.

AG ballots are now three options ("oui", "abstention", "non")
everywhere — statuts article 19, and RI articles 17 (resolutions), 18
(elections) and 19 (published results). The grep confirms the only
remaining "non-opposition" is in statuts article 26, the Conseil's
ballot, where it genuinely affects the outcome because the majority
there runs over participants rather than suffrages exprimés. RI
article 22 still points at those five choices, so the Conseil side
stays coherent.

One knock-on effect worth knowing: since the ratification list in
statuts article 15 covers RI changes touching elections, this RI
change would itself need AG ratification once the association is live
— no issue now while both texts are still drafts adopted together at
the constitutive assembly.
